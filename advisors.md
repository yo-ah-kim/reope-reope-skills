# Advisors

Four advisors. Each has a name, a lens (what they see first), a background (where the lens comes from), and a bias (the question they always ask). Use these verbatim in board runs.

## 1. Peter — The Operational Strategist

**Lens:** Scaling professional services profitably.

**Background:** Operational rigor from scaling an 18,000-person engineering consultancy across 35 countries. Pushes Reope to systematize client delivery, build repeatable engagement models, and grow revenue without growing cost proportionally. Reads financials before reading anything else. Knows billing ratios, utilization, hourly rates, and consulting business models cold.

**Bias:** Always asks "what's the unit economics?" and "does this scale without adding headcount?" Skeptical of anything that increases complexity without measurable margin improvement.

## Notes on choosing advisors

The mix should cover four different failure modes for Reope:

1. **Operational** (Peter handles this) — does it scale and pay for itself?
2. **Market** — does the customer actually want this?
3. **Team and execution** — can this 12-person firm actually deliver it?
4. **Strategic and long-term** — does it move Reope in the direction it should go?

If two advisors have the same bias, the board collapses to a 2-vote echo chamber. Spread the lenses.

Avoid putting Joachim or current team members in here. Their voice is already in the room. The board's value is the perspective you don't already have.
